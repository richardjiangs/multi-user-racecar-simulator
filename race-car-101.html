Open Simulator

Drive the simulator like a racer.

Start gently, brake in a straight line, look through the corner, and let the car unwind before full throttle. This guide teaches the habits that make the six hypercars easier to control in practice and online rooms.

101km/h, clean line

First Drive

Pick a car.

Start with private practice. Learn one car before switching, because each one has a different acceleration curve and braking feel.

Power it up.

Press the simulator's start button, select a drive gear, then use small throttle inputs before trying full launch control.

Choose a track.

Use Circuit mode to pick a route. Smooth circuits are better for learning; long tracks reward patience and rhythm.

Build speed slowly.

One clean lap at 70 percent teaches more than five crashed laps at 100 percent.

Cornering

BrakeFinish most braking before turn-in. If the car will not rotate, you are probably still asking too much from the front tires.
ApexAim for the inside middle of the corner. Touch it once, then let the car drift back outward naturally.
ExitThrottle comes back as steering comes off. Full throttle with full steering is how fast cars become slow cars.

Online Race

The host controls the track and race start. Joiners should wait for the host's selection, line up during the five red lights, and launch only when the lights go out.

Host creates the room.

Share the short room code. Keep the host tab open so the room stays alive.

Everyone joins.

Online mode replaces AI rivals with connected human racers only.

Host sets the track.

The selected circuit syncs to every joined racer.

Start clean.

Hold position during the countdown. The first corner is won by patience, not panic.

Track Choice

SilverstoneBest learning track. Wide, readable, and good for braking practice.
SuzukaGood for rhythm. Respect the esses and wait before full throttle.
MonacoSlow hands, early braking, no hero moves. Walls punish impatience.
NardoUse it to understand top speed, stability, and high-speed steering.
Nurburgring GPGreat for learning heavy braking zones and exits.
NordschleifeLong, fast, and unforgiving. Learn it in sections.

Troubleshooting

Car spins on exitUse less throttle until the steering wheel is straighter.
Missed the cornerBrake earlier. Late braking is only useful if you still make the apex.
Cannot join a roomCheck the room code, keep the host page open, and retry if the relay path is slow.
